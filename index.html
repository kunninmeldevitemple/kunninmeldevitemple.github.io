Tucked away in the serene landscapes of Kottayam, Kiliroor Kunninmel Devi Temple (കിളിരൂർ കുന്നിന്‍മേല്‍ ദേവി ക്ഷേത്രം) is a spiritual beacon for devotees. Perched on a gentle hill, this ancient shrine is dedicated to Goddess Parvati, who is revered as the divine mother and a source of peace, prosperity, and protection. Its tranquil setting and traditional Kerala architecture create a truly sacred space for prayer and reflection.

This website provides a glimpse into the temple's history, traditions, and festivals. We hope it assists you in your journey to this sacred place.

About the Temple

The Kiliroor Kunninmel Devi Temple is steeped in history and folklore, with its origins believed to date back several centuries. The presiding deity, Goddess Parvati, is worshipped in her tranquil form, embodying maternal love and divine power. The temple's architecture showcases traditional Kerala style, with its intricate woodwork and a serene sanctum sanctorum.

Beyond its religious significance, the temple is also a cultural hub, hosting various festivals throughout the year. The annual festival, a spectacle of vibrant colors and traditional art forms, attracts devotees from all over the region.

Mythological Significance

Legend has it that the Goddess chose this sacred hilltop as her abode to bless and protect the local community. The temple is a symbol of faith and a place where countless generations have found solace and spiritual strength.

Other Deities

In addition to the primary deity, the temple complex houses several other shrines dedicated to various Hindu deities. These sub-deities are also revered by devotees and are an integral part of the temple's spiritual life.

  • Lord Shiva: The temple has a dedicated shrine for Lord Shiva.
  • Lord Ganapathy: Devotees offer prayers to Lord Ganesha for the removal of obstacles.
  • Lord Sastha: A shrine for Lord Sastha, also known as Lord Ayyappa, is present.
  • Yakshi: The temple also has a shrine for Yakshi.
  • Lord Srikrishna: Uniquely, the temple also has statues depicting Buddha in the form of Lord Srikrishna.

Major Festivals

  • Annual Ulsavam (Meenam): An 8-day celebration culminating in the **Arattu** ceremony on the day of Pooram nakshatra, typically held in the Malayalam month of Meenam (March–April).
  • Navaratri Mahotsavam (Kanni month): A nine-day festival in honor of Goddess Bhagavathy, featuring special rituals, cultural programs, and vibrant processions.

Pooja & Timings

Daily Timings

  • Temple opens: 5:00 AM (Nada Thurakkal)
  • Nirmalyam: 5:10 AM
  • Ganapathy Homam: 5:30 AM
  • Sreebali: 7:00 AM
  • Usha Pooja: 7:30 AM
  • Ucha (noon) Pooja: 9:30 AM
  • Temple closes: around 10:00 AM

Evening Timings

  • Temple reopens: ~5:30 PM
  • Deeparadhana: 6:30 PM
  • Athazha (evening) Pooja: 7:00 PM
  • Temple closes: around 7:30 PM

Pooja Offerings

  • Archana (Special prayer)
  • Pushpanjali (Flower offering)
  • Thrikkaduva Pooja (Daily rituals)
  • Ganapathi Homam (For prosperity)
  • Swayamvara Parvathi Homam (For marriage blessings)
  • Charakkil Ada: Adorning the deity with sweet ada made using 101 units each of rice, coconut, banana, 3 thulam jaggery, and ghee (neyyu).
